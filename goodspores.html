Good Spores

Overview

Good Spores is an organic mushroom supplement company that supplies an arrangement of products such as powders, tinctures, capsules, coffees and teas. The objective was to develop the brand, design the logo and create the responsive web application for the purpose of selling products and educating consumers about the health benefits of medicinal mushrooms.

First I needed to develop the brand guidelines. It needed to be clean, easy to navigate, a dynamic color palette, a simple logo with complimentary typography reminiscent of the 70’s and a semi-professional, yet fun writing style.

Next, my focus was on constructing the user-flow to build the web application.

Having the structure built, I had a vision for how I wanted the design layout to look. I wanted every screen to be convey simplicity and avoid clutter.

Once the base design was established, I then went on to designing the mid-fidelity wireframes to further visualize application functionality. At this point, user testing was introduced to obtain feedback.

After digesting feedback, the implementation of new ideas went into the construction of the high fidelity wireframes.

The final product was then fine tuned for aesthetic appeal and visual cohesion.

Design Process

User Flow

Considering that mushroom supplements are fairly new, I wanted to make sure the site was extremely simple, educational and easy to navigate.

Low Fidelity

This the beginning of organizing the overall structure, I was focused on keeping it simple and easy to navigate.

Mid Fidelity

My vision was becoming stronger at this stage of the design process. The only screen that I wasn’t confident about was the home screen. The home screen will go through different iterations before the final design.

I conducted user tests during this stage before moving on to finalizing the design. I received positive navigation feedback from all my user tests which gave me confidence in the functionality of my app. One user, however, suggested placing the company logo within the top navigation bar rather than, the name “Good Spores.” Another piece of constructive advice was to allow more breathing room between the product images on the powder product screen. I took the constructive criticism and implemented it into the next stage of my design.

High Fidelity

Final thoughts

Good Spores taught me a lot about the impact of branding and the challenges of keeping things simple. I felt that the color scheme, typeface and logo all worked cohesively to achieve that goal. I wanted the home screen to be the most impactful in order to draw first time users into exploring further. The final design choice was to highlight the best sellers with the mushroom illustrations and to communicate the ability to “shop by benefits” at the bottom, to assist new users. Further development would include the option of a monthly subscriptions box and an ongoing educational blog. Overall the design conveys exactly what I envisioned.
